Traversing dense vegetation is crucial for unlocking a wide range of UAV field applications, yet it remains highly challenging: dense wilderness obstacles offer very limited free space, often narrower than the UAV itself, making such environments practically untraversable with existing methods. This requires UAVs to either proactively modify the environment to create traversable space or adapt themselves to fit through it. We propose a new approach that enhances UAV traversability in the extremely dense wild through contact-driven adaptation to the environment. Within a unified framework, our method enables three phenomena for handling diverse obstacles: (1) Push Aside — when encountering deformable obstacles like tree branches and leaves, the UAV pushes them aside to create navigable space; (2) Pose Adjustment — upon contact with rigid obstacles, the UAV adjusts its pose to squeeze through; and (3) adaptive interaction across mixed environments.
